  • Women's Suffrage Movement


    • WSM Timeline



    1848:The first convention is held at Seneca Falls.

    1850:The convention moves to Worcester gaining one thousand plus participants.

    1868: The 14th Amendment is ratified.

    1869: The movement is split between Susan B. Anthony and Elizabeth Cady Stanton.

    1870: The 15th Amendment is ratified.

    1872: Susan B. Anthony us arrested.

    1878: The WSM is first introduced to Congress.

    1890: The National Women's Suffrage Association and The American Women's Suffrage Association merge forming the National American Women Suffrage Association (NAWSA).

    1893: Colorado is the first to accept the amendment allowing women the right to vote.

    1896: The National Association of Colored Women is formed.

    1913: The Congressional Union for Women Suffrage is formed by Alice Paul and Lucy Burns.

    1916: The National Women's Party (NWP) is formed by Alice Paul and her colleagues.

    1917: Alice Paul and other July protesters were arrested for obstructing traffic.

    1818: President Wilson announces women's suffrage was needed as a "war measure".

    1819: The Federal Woman Suffrage Amendment was passed by the House of Representatives and Senate and is sent to the state for ratification.

    August 26, 1920: The 19th Amendment of the Constitution is signed into a law granting women the right to finally vote.
